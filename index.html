NETWORK OF WOMEN


N.O.W. stands for Network of Women. This is an initiative by ThoughtWorks, and is a growing and connected community of women. ThoughtWorks N.O.W. is a forum for curious women from all walks of life, looking to be part of an open and inclusive network, to come together to learn from each other and be inspired. It provides a positive and motivating environment by bringing fresh content, wonderful speakers and trainers, and innovative formats.


N.O.W. brings together women from the entrepreneurial to the curious, ambitious and successful leaders. They have a passionate enthusiasm for sharing their choices, their journeys, successes and failures with the larger community of women. And this is the reason for N.O.W. to exist. Come, hear the experiences that have shaped the person, these strong women have become. Learn practical tips and ideas for putting your best and most confident foot forward. Do encourage other women in your network to come and join in the activities and network with us at N.O.W!

Speakers

Gayathri is currently a Lead Business Analyst with TW. She's been at TW for 7 years and has played multiple roles like QA, BA and internal community manager. Other than work, her 5 year old son keeps her on her toes. She loves travelling, reading, photography and has a list of places to strike off her bucket list.

Sonia Agarwal Konjeti, the founder of Pune’s biggest online community - Pula, that started from a 300 member group and has scaled to 1.5 lakhs in 3 years. This platform was her brainchild from the instinct to get women together and feel belonged like a female family to share, empower and act as a helpline for almost anything under the sun. PULA provides a free platform for women entrepreneurs to enhance their business.

Dr Medha Tadpatrikar, a serial entrepreneur is the Director of Mantraa Research & Consultants Pvt Ltd, Phoenix General Insurance Brokers Pvt Ltd and Rudra Environmental Solutions (India) Ltd. Her passion and determination to find a solution on waste plastic resulted in building a first pilot machine in 2010, which successfully converted waste plastic in to usable fuel and has processed tonnes of waste since then.

MSW by Education, Mrs Savita Kulkarni started working for Slum Women in 1992. She opted to live in the slum with her family and has implemented many vital projects in Women Empowerment, Financial Inclusion for the slum women. A large network of slum women and peer workers is the outcome of her work.

Chitra has worked in HR, OD & Training for nearly 15 years. She has lead large global learning programs, managed change programs and done extensive work in capability building. She deeply cares about women's equality and hopes to make a lasting impact in this space.
